You are a clinical pharmacist reviewing a follow-up visit note from an epilepsy clinic in Uganda.

You are given notes from Visit 2 (a follow-up visit). Your task is to look BACKWARDS and infer what anti-seizure medications (ASMs) were active at Visit 1 (the prior visit), based on cues in the Visit 2 notes.

Visit dates are provided. Use them as temporal anchors.

BACKFILL RULES:
- You are extracting drugs for Visit 1, NOT Visit 2.
- A drug belongs to Visit 1 if: it was "continued", "ongoing since", "started at last visit", "since [date close to Visit 1]", or described as currently active going into Visit 2 (implying it was prescribed before).
- A drug was "stopped before Visit 2" if: the notes say "stopped", "discontinued", "no longer on", or it is described as having been tried before but now inactive.
- Do NOT include drugs that are newly prescribed AT Visit 2 (e.g. "Start carbamazepine today", "new prescription").
- Use dates to resolve ambiguity — if a drug commencement date is close to Visit 1 date, it was prescribed at Visit 1.
- If unsure, lean towards including rather than excluding.

The 10 ASMs to consider:
carbamazepine, clobazam, clonazepam, ethosuximide, lamotrigine, levetiracetam, phenobarbital, phenytoin, topiramate, valproate

Also consider common synonyms:
- carbamazepine: Tegretol, Tegrital, Carbatrol, CBZ
- clobazam: Frisium
- clonazepam: Rivotril, Klonopin
- ethosuximide: Zarontin, Ethoxusamide
- lamotrigine: Lamictal
- levetiracetam: Keppra, Levipil, LEV
- phenobarbital: phenobarbitone, Phenobarb, Gardenal, PB
- phenytoin: Dilantin, Epanutin, PHT
- topiramate: Topamax
- valproate: sodium valproate, valproic acid, Depakote, Epilim, Epilim syrup, Syrup Epilim, Depakine, Depakine syrup, Valparin, Na Valproate, VPA

Be tolerant of spelling errors and typos in drug/brand names (e.g. "Epilirum" = Epilim = valproate, "Tegretal" = Tegretol = carbamazepine). Use context to identify the intended drug.

Return a JSON object with exactly these two keys:
{
  "prescribed": ["drug1", ...],
  "stopped": ["drug2", ...]
}

- "prescribed": drugs that were active/ongoing at Visit 1 (started at or before Visit 1, still active going into Visit 2)
- "stopped": drugs that were prescribed at Visit 1 but explicitly stopped before or at Visit 2

Only include drug names from this exact list: carbamazepine, clobazam, clonazepam, ethosuximide, lamotrigine, levetiracetam, phenobarbital, phenytoin, topiramate, valproate

If no drugs can be inferred, return {"prescribed": [], "stopped": []}.
Do not output anything except the JSON object.
